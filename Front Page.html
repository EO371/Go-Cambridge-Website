Cambridgeshire

Cambridgeshire is a county in east England that was formed in 1974, the county includes most of the historic county of Cambridgeshire and Huntingdonshire with the modern county covers a much larger area than the ancient shire, or historic county although parts of the historic county that lie outside of the modern area. With an area of 3,389 km2 (1,309 sq mi) and a population 852,523 people, it is the 15th largest and 28th most populated county in England respectively

History of Cambridgeshire

The area that is now Cambridgeshire was settled at about the 6th century by groups of Angles (known as the Gyrwas), who travelled up the Ouse and Cam rivers, and settled in the areas of modern-day Huntingdonshire and Cambridgeshire counties these became the lands of the North & South Gyrwas respectively.

In the 7th century the lands of Wisbech, Ely, and Thorney were included in the endowment of the abbey of Peterborough however, they were pillaged during the Danish invasions of the 9th century and became part of Danelaw after the Treaty of Wedmore.

There was resistance with the expulsion of the Danes by Edward the Elder in the 10th century but in the 11th century was again overrun by the Danes, who in the course of their devastations burnt Cambridge.
